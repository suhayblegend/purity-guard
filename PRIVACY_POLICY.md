## 2. How Your Data is Stored

All data related to Purity Guard is stored in one place: **locally on your computer**. Everything is under your complete control.

### Local Storage (Default)

By default, all your extension settings and data are stored **locally on your device** using your browser's built-in storage API. **We cannot and do not access this information.**

This locally-stored data includes:
- ‚úÖ Your custom blocklist and whitelist
- ‚úÖ Your streak counters and statistics
- ‚úÖ Victory journal entries
- ‚úÖ All extension settings and preferences
- ‚úÖ Sadaqah (charity) pledge amount and payment history
- ‚úÖ Telegram bot token and chat IDs (if you use the accountability feature)

### Chrome Sync (Optional)

If you enable **Chrome Sync** in your browser settings, some extension data may automatically sync across your devices through **Google's Chrome Sync service**. This is entirely controlled by Google, not by us. We have no access to your synced data. You can disable Chrome Sync at any time in your browser settings.

## 4. Permissions Explained

Purity Guard requests only the **minimum permissions necessary** to function. Here's why we need each one:

| Permission | Purpose | Privacy Impact |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **tabs** | To read the current tab's URL and redirect you if it matches your blocklist | We never store or transmit URLs |
| **storage** | To save your settings (blocklist, streak, etc.) locally on your device | All data stays on your device |
| **webNavigation** | To detect harmful URLs before the page loads, enabling instant blocking | No data is collected |
| **declarativeNetRequest** | Modern, privacy-safe Chrome API used to block harmful websites at the network level | No browsing data is accessed |
| **alarms** | To run periodic tasks like the 2-minute reflection timer and daily streak updates | No data is collected |
| **notifications** | To show optional reminders and motivational messages | You control this in settings |

**None of these permissions are used to collect, store, or transmit your personal data.**
